What is an RSS feed?

RSS has been around since the start - a website can update their RSS feed whenever they, for example, publish a newsletter, and anyone 'listening' to it, can get updated. It's different to an email subscription, where the website pushes notifications out, instead, the reader has to check in on the feed regularly and see if there is an update.

Why use an RRS feed?

As I outline in the philosophy of the website, publishing to my RSS feed allows me to avoid collecting your data! If I start looking after a list of email addresses, it could get hacked! By just listening to my RSS feed, I don't have to know anything about you, and I can't sell your data to advertisers. I really recommend you get into RSS generally, because it's a great way to consume content in this enshittified Internet.

How do I subscribe?

You need to use some software which is able to listen to RSS feeds. I recommend the app 'Feedly' for your phone. Put in the RSS link above and you can select if you want to get notified whenever I update it. You might need to 'fetch full article' depending on which app you use.

What's going into the newsletter?

I will aggregate all the new content, so I'm talking films, games, books etc, from the last time I sent a newsletter and put it in the post. I'll also give a short life update for your enjoyment as well. Maybe I'll have some other thoughts about life to share.
